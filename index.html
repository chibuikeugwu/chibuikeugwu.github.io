About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Washington State University, advised by Prof. Jana Doppa. My research is in artificial intelligence and machine learning, with a focus on robust, safe, and trustworthy ML algorithms with theoretical guarantees for safety-sensitive domains (e.g., healthcare). My current work includes:

  • Building trustworthy Large Language Model (LLM) hallucination control policies.
  • Advancing conformal prediction-based uncertainty quantification with theoretical guarantees to facilitate effective human–ML collaboration (e.g., Clinician-in-the-Loop), including deployed prototypes for UTI detection and beyond.
  • Developing uncertainty-aware energy management methods for wearable Internet of Things (IoT) devices for mobile health applications and beyond.

Projects

I advance conformal prediction-based uncertainty quantification with theoretical guarantees for human–ML collaboration, including software prototypes such as APR, URIC, and Clinician-in-the-Loop Conformal-Calibrated Intervals (CCI) for UTI risk detection in smart homes.

I develop policies and frameworks to detect, quantify, and control hallucinations in large language models, enabling safer deployment in human-facing and safety-critical applications.

I design uncertainty-aware energy management methods for resource-constrained wearable IoT devices, using conformal prediction to forecast ambient energy harvesting with provable coverage guarantees.
