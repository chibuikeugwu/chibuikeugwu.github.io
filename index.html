About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Washington State University, where I am fortunate to be advised by professors Jana Doppa and Yan Yan. My general research interests are in machine learning and artificial intelligence, with a focus on developing robust, safe, and trustworthy deep learning algorithms and theoretical frameworks for safety-sensitive domains. My current work includes:

  • Building trustworthy Large Language Model (LLM) hallucination control policies.
  • Adapting calibration-based uncertainty quantification methods, like conformal prediction, to facilitate effective and secure human–ML collaboration.
  • Developing uncertainty-aware energy management methods for wearable Internet of Things (IoT) devices.

Projects

Conformal prediction for human–ML collaboration

I adapt calibration-based uncertainty quantification methods—especially conformal prediction—to produce reliable prediction sets and uncertainty regions that support effective and secure collaboration between humans and machine learning systems.

Trustworthy LLM hallucination control

I develop policies and frameworks to detect, quantify, and control hallucinations in large language models, enabling safer deployment in human-facing and safety-critical applications.

Uncertainty-aware wearable IoT energy management

I design uncertainty-aware energy management methods for resource-constrained wearable IoT devices, using conformal prediction to forecast ambient energy harvesting with provable coverage guarantees.
Highlighted papers:
Sustainable Wearables for Health Applications (IJCAI 2025)
Uncertainty-Aware Energy Management for Wearable IoT (DAC 2025)

Multi-target cognitive health from smartwatch data

I develop methods such as Uncertainty Regions via Importance-weighted Calibration (URIC) to construct compact, interpretable multi-target prediction regions for cognitive health measures from continuous smartwatch sensor data.
